📖 2 Corinthians 5:21

“He made Him who knew no sin to be sin in our behalf, so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him.”

🕵️ 1. Observation: What does the verse say?

  • "He made Him" – God the Father did something.
  • "Who knew no sin" – Jesus, who was sinless.
  • "To be sin" – Jesus took sin upon Himself.
  • "In our behalf" – for our sake.
  • "So that we might become" – we receive something.
  • "The righteousness of God" – accepted before God.
  • "In Him" – possible only in Jesus.

This is a short but powerful summary of the Gospel.

💡 2. Interpretation: What does it mean?

  • Jesus was perfectly sinless.
  • Jesus was treated as if He were sin itself.
  • This was done for us – for our benefit.
  • We become the righteousness of God in Him.

This verse teaches substitution and our new identity in Christ.

🧰 3. Application: How should I respond?

If you feel unworthy or guilty, remember this: Jesus took your sin and gave you His righteousness.

God's love is already given through Jesus. You don’t need to earn it. Stop living in shame. You are accepted in Christ.

Ask yourself:

  • Do I really believe that I am fully accepted by God in Christ?
  • How can I live today in light of this “great exchange”?

🙏 Short Prayer

Father, thank You for loving me so much that You sent Jesus to take my place. He took my sin so I could receive His righteousness. Help me to never forget this gift. Let me live each day knowing I am accepted, forgiven, and loved – not because of what I’ve done, but because of what Jesus did. In His name I pray, Amen.
